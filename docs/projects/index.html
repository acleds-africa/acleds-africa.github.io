Longitudinal Mathematics Learning Analytics Study

LMLAS

The LMLAS is a multi-year tracking study examining how mathematics confidence, identity, and measurable achievement develop across the primary and lower secondary school years — using the methods of learning analytics to surface patterns that cross-sectional research cannot capture.

The study follows cohorts of learners over multiple years, combining standardised assessments, teacher observations, learner surveys, and administrative data. The result is a rich longitudinal dataset that can support multiple lines of analysis.

Design

Longitudinal, mixed methods

Themes

Mathematics education, Learning analytics

Status

Data collection ongoing

Duration

Multi-year

Research Questions

  1. How do mathematics confidence and achievement co-evolve across the primary school years?
  2. What learner, teacher, and school factors predict positive mathematics trajectories?
  3. How does mathematical identity interact with achievement outcomes over time?
  4. What do learning analytics approaches reveal that traditional assessment cannot?
