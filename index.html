Flagship Study

Longitudinal Mathematics Learning Analytics Study

A multi-year tracking study examining how mathematics confidence, identity, and achievement co-evolve across primary and secondary schooling — using learning analytics methods to surface patterns invisible to cross-sectional research.

  • Longitudinal design tracking cohorts over multiple years
  • Quantitative and qualitative data integration
  • Direct policy and curriculum implications

Our Purpose

Research Rooted in African Contexts

Too much educational research applied to African schools originates elsewhere. ACLEDS exists to generate knowledge from within — producing scholarship that is contextually valid, methodologically rigorous, and policy-relevant.

We work at the intersection of data science and education to surface insights that can genuinely change what happens in classrooms.

About ACLEDS
01

Contextually Valid

Research designed for and conducted in African school systems, not transplanted from elsewhere.

02

Methodologically Rigorous

Longitudinal designs, mixed methods, and transparent data practices that meet international standards.

03

Policy-Relevant

Findings translated into clear implications for curriculum, teaching practice, and education policy.
